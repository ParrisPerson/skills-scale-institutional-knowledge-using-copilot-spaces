# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Collaboration Boundaries
- Developers own implementation decisions within approved designs; changes to scope or requirements should flow through the Product Manager.
- Coordinate with UX Designers before starting UI work to ensure design specs are finalized.
- Provide Technical Writers with feature context and review drafts for accuracy.

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Collaboration Boundaries
- Product Managers own the "what" and "why"; Project Managers own the "how" and "when."
- Partner with Business Analysts to translate business requirements into actionable specifications.
- Engage Customer Success Managers to incorporate customer feedback into prioritization decisions.

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Collaboration Boundaries
- Project Managers own delivery coordination; Product Managers own scope and backlog prioritization.
- Ensure all cross-functional roles (UX Designer, Technical Writer, Business Analyst, Customer Success Manager) are identified and engaged at project kickoff.
- Escalate unresolved cross-role conflicts to the appropriate stakeholder.

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on user experience and usability, translating requirements into intuitive interfaces, and advocate for the end-user in design decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Collaborate with Product Managers on requirements to ensure user needs are reflected
- Run design reviews and usability tests
- Hand off finalized design specs to developers
- Iterate on designs based on user feedback and usability findings

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support task completion
- Ensure design decisions are validated with real user feedback

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test reports and design handoff documentation
- Feedback loops after release to drive iterative improvements

### Collaboration Boundaries
- UX Designers own the user experience design; Developers own technical implementation of those designs.
- Design changes impacting scope should be coordinated with the Product Manager before handoff.
- Work with Technical Writers to ensure in-product language and documentation are consistent.

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers document features, processes, and release notes to ensure clarity for both internal and external stakeholders. They make complex information accessible and maintainable.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain user-facing and developer documentation
- Draft release notes and change logs
- Ensure documentation is accurate, up-to-date, and accessible
- Review and edit content contributed by other team members for clarity

### Goals
- Provide clear, accurate, and timely documentation for every release
- Reduce support burden by proactively addressing common user questions
- Maintain a single source of truth for internal and external documentation

### Typical Communication
- Collaboration with Product Managers and Developers during feature development
- Documentation review cycles before each release
- Feedback solicitation from Customer Success Managers on documentation gaps

### Collaboration Boundaries
- Technical Writers own documentation quality and structure; Developers and Product Managers are responsible for providing accurate source information.
- Coordinate with the Project Manager to ensure documentation tasks are included in release timelines.
- Engage Customer Success Managers to identify the most impactful documentation gaps.

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts analyze business processes, elicit requirements, and turn business needs into actionable tasks and specifications. They serve as a bridge between business stakeholders and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze and map current business processes
- Propose workflow and process improvements
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements
- Maintain requirements traceability throughout the project lifecycle

### Goals
- Ensure business needs are clearly understood and accurately reflected in project requirements
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or incomplete requirements
- Support continuous process improvement across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Business requirements documents (BRDs) and process maps
- Review sessions with Product Managers and Developers to align on specifications

### Collaboration Boundaries
- Business Analysts own requirements gathering and documentation; Product Managers own final prioritization.
- Escalate conflicting stakeholder requirements to the Product Manager for resolution.
- Coordinate with the Project Manager when requirement changes impact scope, timeline, or resources.

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers champion customer needs and adoption post-release, monitoring satisfaction and surfacing product feedback to the team to support continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Support customer onboarding and adoption of new features
- Track and respond to customer issues and escalations
- Relay product feedback and customer insights to Product and Project Managers
- Monitor customer satisfaction metrics and report trends

### Goals
- Maximize customer adoption, satisfaction, and retention
- Ensure customer feedback continuously informs the product roadmap
- Reduce time-to-value for customers after each release

### Typical Communication
- Regular check-ins with customers and internal stakeholders
- Customer feedback summaries and escalation reports shared with Product Managers
- Coordination with Technical Writers on documentation gaps identified through customer support

### Collaboration Boundaries
- Customer Success Managers own post-release customer relationships; Product Managers own roadmap decisions based on that feedback.
- Escalate critical customer issues to the Project Manager for triage and resolution.
- Coordinate with Technical Writers to ensure documentation addresses recurring customer questions.

## Role Assignment Checklist

Use this checklist at project kickoff or during onboarding to confirm all necessary roles are identified and appropriately assigned.

- [ ] **Developer(s)** identified and assigned to the project
- [ ] **Product Manager** identified and responsible for backlog and scope
- [ ] **Project Manager** identified and responsible for delivery coordination
- [ ] **UX Designer** engaged (or explicitly confirmed as not required for this project)
- [ ] **Technical Writer** engaged (or explicitly confirmed as not required for this project)
- [ ] **Business Analyst** engaged (or explicitly confirmed as not required for this project)
- [ ] **Customer Success Manager** engaged (or explicitly confirmed as not required for this project)
- [ ] All roles have been introduced to the team and understand their collaboration boundaries
- [ ] Cross-functional touchpoints (design reviews, documentation reviews, requirements workshops) are scheduled or planned
